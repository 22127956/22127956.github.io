Android

Android phones are smartphones that operate using the Android operating system (OS), a flexible, open-source software developed by Google and based on the Linux kernel. They are made by a wide range of manufacturers, offering diverse choices in features and price points.

Operating System: Android is the world's most popular mobile operating system. It is highly customizable, allowing users to personalize their home screens, themes, widgets, and more. The latest public release is Android 15, with Android 16 released to developers.

Hardware Diversity: The open nature of Android means devices come in a vast array of hardware specifications and price ranges, from budget-friendly options to high-end flagship models with advanced cameras and 5G capabilities.

Google Services: Android devices integrate seamlessly with Google's suite of services, including the Google Play Store (offering over 3 million apps), Google Assistant, Google Maps, Gmail, YouTube, and Google Drive..
